Project 3 | Face Morphing

Overview

The goal of Project 3 was to take the correspondence points from one face and morph it into another. It shows you how you can compare 2 faces, or even derive patterns from populations of people.

Approach

Facial keypoints and the Triangulation Mesh

Facial Keypoints

To create the facial keypoints of my and George Clooney’s face, I used the online tool created by a past CS 180 student. I roughly outlined the head, eyes, nose bridge, mouth, jawline, and cheeks.

Triangulation Mesh

After importing the JSON files and extracting the points, I generated the triangulation mesh by using scipy.spatial.Delaunay.

Midway Face

Affine Transformation Matrix

In order to get the Affine Transformation Matrix, given a matrix of homogeneous coordinates T1 and T2, I inverted T1 and matrix multiplied it with T2. The convention I was following was transforming T1 into T2.

Results

Morph Sequence

To create the morph sequence, I generalized the code that I wrote for the previous part. I then iterated through warp and dissolve factors from [0,1] with a step size of 0.05 to create 20 frames.

Mean Face of Danes’ Females

To get the mean face of the females in the Danes dataset, I first imported the .asf files and iterated through each line to extract the x and y coordinates of the annotated correspondence points. I then took the mean of all of those points to create the average shape, producing the triangulation with it.

Note: Warping my face to the average face or vice versa required me to edit the photos so that their dimensions were the same, roughly align the faces, and re-annotate the images based on the order of the correspondence points.

Caricature
